Course description

Deformable domains are ubiquitous in several areas of research but are still a serious computational challenge. We present a comprehensive approach to the formulation and discretization of geometric PDE governing processes relevant in biophysics and material science.

We start with key elements of differential geometry and shape differential calculus which enable us to compute (first) variations of domain and boundary functionals. These tools are used in shape optimization, which is briefly discussed. We introduce the mathematical framework of gradient flows and their approximations. The latter is applied to geometric PDEs leading to the so-called geometric gradient flows proposed as a relaxation toward equilibrium. We derive the corresponding dynamic equations together with their finite element approximations. We apply this framework to mean curvature flow, surface diffusion, Helfrich flow (Willmore flow with area and volume constraints), and large bending deformations with isometry constraint.

This class is at the intersection of Partial Differential Equations, Differential Geometry and Numerical Approximation via Finite Elements. However, no a-priori knowledge is needed as the required tools are going to be introduced in class. In particular, no particular knowledge in differential geometry or finite element methods is required.
