Course description and prerequisites

Credit 3.0. This is a one-semester introductory course on partial differential equations. It is impossible to cover all the material provided in the textbook and some material will be omitted. We will concentrate on classical explicit solution techniques for linear differential equations and understanding the properties of the solutions thus obtained. Specific types of solution methods include separation of variables, Fourier techniques, Green's functions, and the method of characteristics. Classification of partial differential equations and differences between properties of solutions of PDE in various classes will also be an important theme in the course. We will cover most of Chapters 1-4 and 12 of the text, along with portions of Chapters 9 and 10 as time allows. Prerequisite: MATH 308 or MATH 451 or approval of instructor.

Minimal Outcome

Formulation and solution of partial differential equations of mathematical physics; Fourier series and transform methods, complex variable methods, methods of characteristics and first order equations.
