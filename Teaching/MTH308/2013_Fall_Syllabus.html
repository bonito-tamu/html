Course description and prerequisites

Credit 3.0. Ordinary differential equations, solutions in series, solutions using Laplace transforms, systems of differential equations. Prerequisites: MATH 251 or equivalent; knowledge of computer algebra system.
It is impossible to cover all the material provided in the textbook and some material will be omitted.

Minimal Outcome

This course is to provide students with quantitative and problem-solving skills for first order, linear second order and systems of linear differential equations. At the conclusion of this course, students should be able to:
  • Understand the concepts of solution, initial value problem and direction field.
  • Solve first order linear differential equations.
  • Solve second order homogeneous and non-homogeneous linear ODEs.
  • Solve system of linear equations.
  • Derive qualitative behavior of solutions nonlinear ODEs.
  • Understand and apply basic numerical methods for solving.

Homeworks, Quizzes and Matlab Project

Homework will be assigned weekly every Saturday but not collected. They will not be announced in class but be posted on the class web site one week prior the due date. I encourage you to discuss homework problems with your classmates, including strategies for solving different kinds of problems. The purpose of these homework assignments is that you obtain practice in the techniques discussed in class. Solving the problems diligently is therefore a vital component of your learning experience and will also prepare you for the quizzes and exams. Help sessions and week in review are offered by the department of mathematics. However, the schedule for the week in review may not correspond to the topics discussed in class.

On a weekly basis on the due dates (usually on Tuesday), we will have a 5-minute quiz (closed book, closed notes) with problem closely related to assigned homework.

Due to new Texas A&M regulation, the quizzes and exams are not returned in class anymore. Instead, you are welcome to pick your copies up during office hours. However, note that any discussion on your grade will be only possible within 3 weeks (starting at the quiz date).

The weekly homeworks may contain matlab exercises. I strongly encourage you to consider them to gain insights on the behavior of ODEs. Moreover, an optional take-home matlab project will be assigned on due time. Students can work in groups of at most three. There is no report to return but the project grade is determined after an oral examination where, in particular, each group will be asked to run and explain the matlab codes and results obtained. Help session for matlab are provided by the department of mathematics, visit Calc Lab.
